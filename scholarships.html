National Merit Scholarship Packages

Up to $70,000+ over four years

Some universities offer extraordinarily generous packages to National Merit Finalists — covering tuition, housing, and additional stipends. Here are three documented examples with sources.

University of Alabama $70,000+ total value

The National Merit Finalist package at Alabama includes:

  • Full tuition for up to 5 years (10 semesters)
  • 4 years of on-campus housing
  • $4,000 per year supplemental scholarship for 4 years
  • $2,000 one-time research or international study allowance

When tuition and housing are combined over multiple years, the total value readily exceeds $70,000.

afford.ua.edu/scholarships/national-merit
University of Oklahoma $73,000–$75,000 total

The University of Oklahoma publishes an official National Merit Finalist Package PDF that explicitly lists the Resident Total value — including tuition waivers, stipends, and housing support.

  • Published resident total: $73,000–$75,000 range over multiple years
  • Includes tuition waiver, housing, and annual stipend

Texas A&M University $13,000–$43,000 total

Texas A&M packages National Merit awards ranging from $13,000 to $43,000 over 4 years of undergraduate study, depending on the specific award tier.

  • Multiple scholarship tiers available to Finalists
  • Upper tier reaches $43,000 over four years

Automatic Merit Scholarships — No Application Needed

Up to $28,000 per year for strong SAT scores

Some public universities publish automatic, stats-based merit scholarships — awarded purely on GPA and test score thresholds, with no separate scholarship application required.

University of Alabama — Automatic Merit Aid Up to $28,000 / year

Alabama's automatic out-of-state merit scholarships are tiered by GPA and SAT/ACT score. The highest tier reaches $28,000 per year for qualifying students.

  • Awarded automatically — no separate scholarship application
  • Available to out-of-state students meeting GPA and test score thresholds
  • Tiers begin at lower score ranges — strong scores increase award amount
collegetracker.app — Alabama Merit Scholarships road2college.com — Alabama Merit Overview

Important context

The $28,000/year figure represents the upper end of published automatic awards — not a guaranteed outcome for every student. At the booth, our team can explain what score ranges are needed for each tier, and how our program builds toward those scores from Grade 7 onward.

Why We Start in Grade 7

The PSAT/NMSQT happens in Grade 11 — the clock starts now

The National Merit Scholarship competition is entered through the PSAT/NMSQT — and only the score earned in 11th grade counts. Earlier tests (PSAT 8/9, PSAT 10) do not qualify students for National Merit. This means preparation must begin years before junior year.

Gr 7–8
Foundation — Math and English
Build strong algebra, reading comprehension, and grammar fundamentals. This is the base that everything else rests on. Students who start here have the most runway.
Gr 9–10
Acceleration — PSAT 8/9 and PSAT 10
These tests do not qualify for National Merit but are excellent practice and progress benchmarks. Students deepen problem-solving skills and begin exam-format practice.
Gr 11
The Qualifying Test — PSAT/NMSQT
This is the test that counts. Only 11th grade PSAT/NMSQT scores are used to determine National Merit Semifinalist status. Students who have prepared since Grade 7 arrive here with a multi-year advantage.
Gr 11–12
SAT / ACT and Scholarship Applications
Strong SAT scores unlock automatic merit scholarships at hundreds of universities. National Merit Finalists begin their scholarship applications and college selection.

The official source

According to College Board, students must take the PSAT/NMSQT during their third year of high school (Grade 11) to enter the National Merit Scholarship Program. Only the score earned in junior year counts toward National Merit eligibility.
