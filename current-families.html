In Person

The Sunday sessions

We meet in person at the Gurudwara. A normal Sunday runs:

  • 10:45 AM
    Assembly — Centering with the Mool Mantar, led by parents.
  • 11:00 AM
    Math & English Quiz — Students complete and submit a Google Form quiz.
  • 11:45 AM
    Quiz Review & Coaching — Tutors evaluate each student's written solution steps using a rubric, guide them through the correct problem-solving process, and provide individualized feedback.
  • 12:10–12:20 PM
    Short Break
  • 12:20–1:00 PM
    Concept Reinforcement — Tutors summarize the key concepts and problem-solving strategies from the session, and students handwrite their notes to reinforce learning.

Our ten weekends: June 14 (first session) · June 21 · June 28 · July 12 · July 19 · August 2 · August 9 · Saturday, August 15 (the whole in-person session moves to Saturday this week — there is no session on Sunday, August 16) · August 23 · August 30 (closing).

No session: July 5 and July 26. The Gurudwara sessions pause on these two Sundays.

On test Sundays, Grades 10 and 11 run later — until 1:30 — so the session can be completed under realistic timing. Please plan pickup accordingly on those days. Your child's grade test dates are below.

Testing

Bluebook test schedule

Every full-length test produces a personalized error log that drives the next weeks of practice. Tests are not about a single score — they show us exactly where to focus.

Each full-length test runs about 2 hours 30 minutes, including a 10-minute break in the middle. Please plan around that on test days, whether in person or at home.

New to Bluebook? Every student needs the Bluebook app installed and their login tested at home before their first practice test. See the step-by-step setup guide →

At Home

Daily practice — what happens at home

Between sessions, students practice every day: 45 minutes of Math and 35 minutes of English. This is the engine of the program. The Sunday session builds on the week's practice; it does not replace it.

How families help most

  • Protect the daily practice time. A consistent, quiet block matters more than a long one.
  • Keep Sunday attendance steady — the sessions build on each other.
  • Stay in contact with your child's tutor. Partner with us on accountability rather than outsourcing it.

Your child's daily practice happens in Khan Academy; Google Classroom is just for the Sunday quizzes (see below).

The Toolset

How the tools fit together

Your child uses a small, deliberate set of tools — each with a clear job.

  • Khan Academy — daily skill practice and lessons. This is where most of the at-home work happens.
  • Google Classroom — where the Sunday quizzes are posted and scored.
  • Bluebook — College Board's official testing app, used for the practice tests above.
  • Desmos — the graphing calculator built into the digital test. Students learn to use it the way they will on test day.

We keep the toolset small on purpose. Fewer tools, used well, beats many tools used shallowly.
